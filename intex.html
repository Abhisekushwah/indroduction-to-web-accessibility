About Web Accessibility

Web accessibility means that websites, tools, and technologies are designed and developed so that people with disabilities can use them. It covers a range of disabilities, including visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, and neurological disabilities.

Accessibility Guidelines

Key Points

  • Provide alternative text for images.
  • Use high-contrast colors for readability.
  • Ensure keyboard navigation is possible.
  • Organize content with headings and landmarks.

Standards We Follow

Standard Description
WCAG 2.1 Guidelines for making web content accessible to people with disabilities.
Section 508 Mandates accessibility of federal websites for people with disabilities.
